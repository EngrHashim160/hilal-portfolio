## Why voice agents are different

Text chatbots are forgiving — latency of a second or two barely registers. Voice agents are not. The moment a conversation feels laggy or robotic, users disengage. Building an automated patient intake agent forced me to treat latency, turn-taking, and reliability as first-class concerns.

## The pipeline

The core loop is straightforward in concept:

1. **LiveKit** handles the real-time audio transport and room management.
2. **Deepgram** provides streaming speech-to-text and text-to-speech.
3. **Groq LLM** drives the conversation logic and decides what information to collect next.

Keeping each hop fast is what makes the conversation feel natural rather than mechanical.

## Wrapping it as a service

The agent and the call-initiation script are wrapped in a **FastAPI** service. This gives a clean HTTP surface for starting calls and integrating with the rest of a practice's systems.

## Shipping it

- **Docker** containerizes the whole agent for reproducible builds.
- **Google Cloud Run** runs it serverlessly, scaling with call volume.
- **GitHub Actions** handles automated CI/CD, so every push that passes goes live.

## Takeaway

A voice agent isn't just a prompt — it's a real-time system. The interesting engineering is in the plumbing: streaming audio, fast LLM responses, and a deployment pipeline that lets you iterate without fear.
